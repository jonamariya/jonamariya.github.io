Black as the devil, hot as hell, pure as an angel, sweet as love!

In 1880 a group of farmers started their own coffee plantations in northern New South Wales and Far North Queensland. Starting with a bang, this first foray into coffee growing resulted with international recognition, winning awards in Paris and Rome.
But the cost of transportation, high labour costs and the Great Depression crippled our coffee industry. In the 1980s, plantations were once again established with the introduction of the mechanical harvester that significantly cut labour costs. Now we have some incredible coffee beans growing in the Northern Rivers (New South Wales) and the Atherton Tablelands (Queensland).
Did you know:
  • Australia is ranked 42nd in the world when it comes to coffee consumption
  • We down about 2.9kg of coffee per capita each year.
  • Our most popular coffee beverages are: cappuccino or flat white all espresso based.
